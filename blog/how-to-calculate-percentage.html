What Is a Percentage?

A percentage is a way of expressing a number as a fraction of 100. The word itself comes from the Latin "per centum," meaning "by the hundred." When we say 25%, we mean 25 out of 100, or the fraction 25/100, or the decimal 0.25. Percentages are everywhere in daily life â€” from shopping discounts and tax rates to exam scores, statistical data, and financial figures.

Understanding percentages is a fundamental life skill. Whether you are calculating how much you save on a sale, figuring out a tip at a restaurant, determining your grade on a test, or analyzing year-over-year business growth, percentage calculations come up constantly.

Common Percentage Mistakes to Avoid

Confusing percentage points with percentages: If an interest rate goes from 5% to 7%, it increased by 2 percentage points, but the percentage increase is actually ((7 - 5) / 5) Ã— 100 = 40%. These are very different concepts and are frequently confused in news reports and discussions.

Asymmetry of increase and decrease: A 50% increase followed by a 50% decrease does not return you to the original value. If $100 increases by 50% to $150, then decreases by 50%, you get $75, not $100. This is because the decrease is applied to the new, larger value.

Dividing by the wrong base: When calculating percentage change, always divide by the original (old) value, not the new value. Dividing by the wrong number gives an incorrect result.

Percentage Shortcuts for Everyday Use

  • 50% = half the number (divide by 2)
  • 25% = quarter the number (divide by 4)
  • 10% = move decimal one place left
  • 1% = move decimal two places left
  • 33.3% = divide by 3
  • Tipping 15%: Find 10%, then add half of that amount
  • Tipping 20%: Find 10% and double it
