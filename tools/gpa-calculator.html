How GPA Is Calculated

Grade Point Average (GPA) is a standardized way of measuring academic achievement in the United States and many other countries. It provides a single number that summarizes a student's overall academic performance. Most institutions use a 4.0 scale, where an A equals 4.0, a B equals 3.0, a C equals 2.0, a D equals 1.0, and an F equals 0.0.

The GPA Formula

To calculate GPA, you multiply the grade point value of each course by its credit hours to get quality points. Then you sum all quality points and divide by the total number of credit hours. Why GPA Matters

GPA is used for scholarship eligibility, graduate school admissions, academic honors, and employment screening. Many scholarships require a minimum GPA of 3.0 or higher. Graduate programs often set a 3.0 or 3.5 minimum for admission. Employers, especially for entry-level positions, sometimes use GPA as a screening criterion. Understanding your GPA helps you set academic goals and track your progress toward them.
