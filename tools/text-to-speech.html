About Text-to-Speech Technology

Text-to-Speech (TTS) is a form of assistive technology that converts written text into spoken words. It has become an integral part of modern computing, powering virtual assistants like Siri, Alexa, and Google Assistant, as well as accessibility features in operating systems, e-readers, and navigation apps.

How It Works in Your Browser

Our Text-to-Speech tool uses the Web Speech API, specifically the SpeechSynthesis interface, which is built into modern web browsers. This means no external services are called ‚Äî the speech is generated using the voices already installed on your device. Different operating systems offer different voice options: Windows provides Microsoft voices, macOS offers Siri voices, and Chrome includes Google's cloud-based voices.

Customization Options

You can customize three key parameters: voice, speed, and pitch. The voice selector lets you choose from any voice available on your system, including different languages and accents. The speed slider (0.5x to 2x) controls how fast the text is read ‚Äî slower speeds are great for language learning, while faster speeds help you consume content quickly. The pitch slider adjusts the tone of the voice from deeper to higher.

Use Cases

  • Accessibility: Help visually impaired users consume written content
  • Proofreading: Hearing your writing read aloud helps catch errors you might miss when reading silently
  • Language Learning: Listen to pronunciation of text in different languages
  • Multitasking: Have articles or emails read to you while doing other tasks
  • Content Creation: Preview how scripts or presentations sound before recording

Privacy

Since the Web Speech API uses your browser's built-in speech engine, your text is processed locally on your device. No data is sent to our servers or any third party. This makes our tool one of the most private text-to-speech solutions available online.
